Background Info:
Alfred Marshall was a British economist who lived from 1842 to 1924. His book "Principles of Economics" (1890) was a foundational text in neoclassical economics. Marshall focused on the concepts of supply, demand, and market equilibrium, and introduced the idea of elasticity of demand.
