Background Info:
Milton Friedman, an American economist and a leading proponent of monetarism, lived from 1912 to 2006. His theories, articulated in works like "Capitalism and Freedom" and "A Monetary History of the United States," emphasize the role of monetary policy in controlling inflation and managing economic cycles. Friedman was a staunch advocate for free markets and minimal government intervention.
