Background Info:
Thorstein Veblen was an American economist and sociologist who lived from 1857 to 1929. He is best known for his book "The Theory of the Leisure Class," which introduced the concept of "conspicuous consumption." Veblen's work critiqued the capitalist economic system and emphasized the social and cultural factors influencing economic behavior.

Use Veblen's theories to discuss the societal and cultural aspects of economics, particularly his concepts of conspicuous consumption and leisure class. Reflect on his critique of capitalism and the role of institutions and evolutionary change in economic life.
